Quantum Computing

What is Quantum Computing?

Quantum computing is an area of computing focused on developing computer technology based on the principles of quantum theory, which explains the behavior of energy and material on the atomic and subatomic levels.

Unlike classical computers, which use bits as the basic unit of information represented by either 0 or 1, quantum computers use quantum bits or qubits. Qubits can represent and store multiple states simultaneously due to quantum superposition and entanglement, allowing quantum computers to perform certain calculations much faster than classical computers.

Applications of Quantum Computing

  • Optimization problems
  • Machine learning and artificial intelligence
  • Cryptography and secure communications
  • Drug discovery and molecular modeling
  • Financial modeling and risk analysis
